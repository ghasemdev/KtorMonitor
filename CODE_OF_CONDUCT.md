# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We, as contributors and maintainers of **KtorMonitor**, pledge to make participation in our project
and community a respectful, inclusive, and harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of
age, body size, disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity and expression, level of
experience, education, socio-economic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or
sexual identity and orientation.

We pledge to act and interact in ways that contribute to an open, welcoming, diverse, and healthy
community.

---

## Our Standards

### Expected Behaviour

- Be respectful and considerate in all communications and code reviews.
- Provide and accept constructive feedback graciously.
- Acknowledge mistakes, learn from them, and move forward.
- Prioritise what is best for the community and the users of this library.
- Show empathy towards other community members.

### Unacceptable Behaviour

- Harassment, intimidation, or discrimination in any form.
- Use of sexualised language or imagery, or unwelcome sexual attention.
- Trolling, insulting/derogatory comments, and personal or political attacks.
- Publishing others' private information without explicit permission.
- Any conduct that would reasonably be considered inappropriate in a professional setting.

---


## Enforcement Responsibilities

Project maintainers are responsible for clarifying and enforcing these standards. They have the
right and responsibility to remove, edit, or reject comments, commits, code, wiki edits, issues, and
other contributions that do not align with this Code of Conduct, and will communicate reasons for
moderation decisions when appropriate.

---

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies within all project spaces (GitHub repository, issues, pull requests,
discussions, and any official communication channels) and also applies when an individual is
officially representing the project in public spaces.

---

## Reporting

Instances of abusive, harassing, unacceptable, or non-compliant behaviour may be reported to the
project maintainers via the contact information listed in the repository profile. All complaints
will be reviewed and investigated promptly and fairly. Maintainers are obligated to respect the
privacy and security of the reporter.

---

## Enforcement Guidelines

Maintainers will follow these guidelines when determining consequences for violations:

| Severity | Example | Consequence |
|----------|---------|-------------|
| **1 — Correction** | Minor style violation or inappropriate comment | Private written warning; clarification of expected behaviour. |
| **2 — Warning** | Single serious incident or repeated minor violations | Formal warning with specified behavioural requirements; temporary suspension of interaction may be applied. |
| **3 — Temporary Ban** | A serious violation or sustained inappropriate behaviour | Temporary ban from the project for a defined period. |
| **4 — Permanent Ban** | Pattern of harassment, threats, or aggression | Permanent removal from the project community. |
